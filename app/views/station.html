What's behind our forecasts?

The Environment Agency considers many things when producing its forecasts. These include the amount of rain that's fallen in the last few days and whether more is expected.

It also looks at likely ground conditions, together with wind, tides and wave height.

When the predicted conditions and river or sea levels combine to threaten flooding, forecasters issue flood alerts or, much more seriously, flood warnings.

Which levels are most likely to indicate flooding?

Flood alerts are commonly associated with levels over 1.6m. They indicate potential flooding of low-lying land such as farmland and recreational fields is likely at:

  • Upper River Ouse catchment

Flood warnings are commonly associated with levels over 2.4m. They indicate that flooding of property is likely at:

  • York city centre
  • York riverside properties
  • York district x

Forecast river and tidal levels are not guaranteed as conditions can change rapidly. We update our forecasts more frequently when there's a greater risk of flooding.
